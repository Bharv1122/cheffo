# Cheffo Doggo — Marketing Plan

*How we get homemade dog food into people's hands (and bowls)*

**App:** https://cheffodoggo.com · **Pricing:** free treat recipe → $8/mo or $59/yr · **Prepared:** July 2026

---

## 1. Positioning

**One-liner:** Personalized, lightly-cooked homemade dog food recipes — approved by *your own* vet, not a celebrity DVM.

The fresh-dog-food wave (Farmer's Dog, Ollie, Nom Nom) proved owners will pay serious money — $150–$300/month — for better food. Cheffo Doggo serves the owners those brands price out: people willing to spend **an hour cooking** instead of $200/month, but who don't trust random Pinterest recipes. Our wedge is trust: toxic-ingredient screening, drug-food interaction checks, AAFCO nutrition targets, and a real veterinarian sign-off workflow. Nobody else in the "DIY" lane has that.

**Who it's for (primary personas):**

- **The New Homemaker** — just adopted or just watched a kibble-recall headline; googling "is homemade dog food safe."
- **The Allergy Parent** — dog with itchy skin / GI issues; elimination diets; reads every label. Highest willingness to pay.
- **The Senior-Dog Caretaker** — dog on medications with a picky appetite; terrified of feeding something that interacts. Our drug-interaction checker is built for them.

## 2. What we already have working for us

- **A free hook with zero friction:** one free treat recipe, no card required. Every marketing channel points here.
- **SEO foundation shipped:** `/learn` answer pages ("Is homemade dog food safe?", "How much homemade food should I feed my dog?", etc.) with FAQ structured data, sitemap, and crawlable static HTML — built for both Google and AI-search citations.
- **A built-in professional viral loop:** every vet-approval request emails a real veterinarian a branded one-page form. Each request is product exposure to the exact professional whose endorsement matters. 100 users requesting approvals = 100 vet clinics that have now seen Cheffo Doggo.
- **Installable PWA** — home-screen app without app-store gatekeeping.
- **Vercel Web Analytics** already wired, so every experiment below is measurable from day one.

## 3. Channels (in priority order)

### 3.1 Short-form video (TikTok / Instagram Reels / YouTube Shorts) — the main engine
Dog content is the most-shared category on every platform, and cooking content is second. We sit at the intersection.

- **Format A — "Cook with me, for my dog":** 30–45s batch-cooking session, dog waiting impatiently, final bowl shot. CTA: "recipe personalized to YOUR dog, link in bio."
- **Format B — "Can my dog eat this?":** rapid-fire safety facts pulled straight from our toxic-food database (grapes, xylitol, onions…). Highly saveable/shareable; positions the app as the safety authority.
- **Format C — "My vet actually approved it":** show the real vet-approval badge and the one-page form. This is the differentiator no competitor can duplicate in a video.
- Cadence: 3–4 posts/week, batch-filmed in one session (the app's own weekly-batch philosophy, applied to content).

### 3.2 Community seeding (Reddit + Facebook groups)
r/DogFood, r/dogs, r/AskVet threads and breed-specific Facebook groups are full of "what do I feed a dog with X?" questions. Answer genuinely, mention the free treat recipe only where relevant. The Allergy Parent persona lives here. Zero cost, high intent.

### 3.3 SEO + AI-search compounding
Keep shipping one `/learn` page per week targeting long-tail questions ("homemade food for dogs with pancreatitis", "dog food recipe with chicken and rice portions by weight"). These pages are already structured for AI assistants to cite — and "best app for homemade dog food" is a page we already own.

### 3.4 Vet-channel flywheel
After a vet approves a recipe, follow up with a one-pager: "Your client used Cheffo Doggo — here's a QR code/handout for your waiting room." Vets constantly get "can I cook for my dog?" questions and have nothing to hand out. We become the answer. Later: a simple referral code per clinic.

### 3.5 Micro-influencer barter
Dog accounts with 10k–100k followers (especially allergy-dog and senior-dog accounts) get a free year of Premium in exchange for one honest cook-along video. Cost: $59 of foregone revenue per creator.

## 4. Funnel

| Stage | Mechanism |
|---|---|
| Discover | Short-form video, Reddit/FB answers, /learn SEO |
| Try | Free treat recipe — no card, ~2 minutes to "wow" |
| Adopt | Dog profile saved → personalized meal preview shown behind paywall |
| Pay | $8/mo or $59/yr (annual pushed at checkout), 14-day guarantee |
| Retain | Weekly batch rhythm, new recipes, Ask Cheffo Doggo, vet badge |
| Refer | Vet loop + shareable recipe cards ("share your dog's menu") |

## 5. 30 / 60 / 90 days

**Days 1–30 — Prove the loop.** Post 12–15 short-form videos across TikTok/Reels/Shorts; find the format that converts. Seed 20 genuine community answers. Ship 4 new /learn pages. Goal: **250 free treat-recipe signups, first 10 paying subscribers, 5 vet approvals requested.**

**Days 31–60 — Double down.** Cut everything but the winning video format and 2x its cadence. Launch the vet waiting-room one-pager to every clinic that has approved a recipe. Recruit 5 micro-influencers. Goal: **1,000 signups, 50 subscribers (~$400 MRR), 25 vet clinics touched.**

**Days 61–90 — Systematize.** Referral incentive ("give a month, get a month"), email drip for free users who never generated a meal recipe, seasonal treat campaigns (Halloween pup-cakes). Goal: **150 subscribers (~$1,200 MRR), free→paid conversion ≥ 5%.**

## 6. Metrics that matter

- Free treat-recipe signups per week (top of funnel)
- Free → paid conversion rate (target 5%+)
- Vet-approval requests per 100 users (the moat metric)
- 60-day retention of paying subscribers (does the batch-cooking habit stick?)
- CAC ≈ $0 for the first 90 days — every channel above is organic/barter

## 7. Budget

$0 required for the first 90 days. Optional accelerants once the funnel converts: $200–500/month on boosting the single best-performing video, and printed waiting-room handouts (~$50/clinic batch).

---

*The core bet: dog owners already trust two things — their own vet, and other dog owners on video. Cheffo Doggo's marketing puts the product inside both of those trust channels instead of buying ads around them.*
