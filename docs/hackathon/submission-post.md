🐕 **Cheffo Doggo — homemade dog food your vet actually signs off on**

**Live app:** https://cheffodoggo.com

Most dog owners who cook for their dogs are winging it — and most "fresh dog food" startups just ship you expensive frozen packs. Cheffo Doggo takes the third path: it builds **personalized, lightly-cooked homemade recipes** you cook yourself from normal grocery ingredients, and then gets them **reviewed by your own veterinarian** — not a celebrity DVM on a landing page.

**How it works:**

1. Add your dog's profile — weight, age, breed, activity level, allergies, even current medications.
2. Pick a recipe type: full meal, weekly batch (cook once, feed all week), kibble topper, treats, or Pantry Mode (build a recipe from what's already in your kitchen).
3. Cook with confidence — step-by-step cooking mode, portions in real kitchen units (cups/tbsp, not grams), a one-tap shopping list, and a vet-approval packet.

**The safety layer is the heart of it.** Every recipe is screened against a toxic-ingredient database AND a drug-food interaction checker (e.g., it hard-blocks fish oil for a dog on blood thinners). The nutrition panel shows per-cup and per-day calories with macros checked against AAFCO life-stage targets. And the vet-approval flow emails your actual vet a one-page form that takes ~60 seconds — the recipe then carries a real "Approved by Dr. ___, DVM" badge with a PDF export.

There's also **Ask Cheffo Doggo**, an AI nutrition assistant that knows your specific dog's profile and gives specific answers ("steam carrots 8–10 minutes," not "cook them").

**Business model:** live Stripe billing — one free treat recipe to try (no card), then $8/mo or $59/yr for full meals, batches, and the assistant. 14-day money-back guarantee.

**Stack:** React 19 + TypeScript + Vite, Tailwind 4, Supabase (auth + Postgres with RLS), Stripe, LLM-backed chat with a vet-grade offline knowledge base fallback, installable PWA, deployed on Vercel.
