# AI Workshop: Curriculum, Teaching Guide & Monetization Plan

A complete plan for launching a paid, beginner-friendly AI workshop — what to teach,
how to teach it, what to charge, and where to sell it.

---

## 1. Positioning: who is this for?

Don't teach "AI" in general — teach an outcome. The people who pay for beginner
workshops are not developers; they are:

- **Small business owners & solopreneurs** who want to save time and make more money
- **Office professionals** worried about falling behind at work
- **Freelancers/creatives** (writers, marketers, realtors, coaches)
- **Curious adults 40+** who feel left out of the AI conversation

**Recommended flagship title:**
> **"AI for Real Life & Business: Do a Week's Work in a Day"**
> A hands-on 3-hour workshop. No tech background needed. Bring a laptop, leave with
> real results.

Outcome-based titles ("save 10 hours a week") sell far better than topic-based ones
("Introduction to Large Language Models").

---

## 2. The flagship: 3-hour hands-on workshop

Three hours is the sweet spot for a paid local workshop: long enough to feel
substantial and justify $49–$149, short enough for a weeknight or Saturday morning.

### Session outline

**Part 1 — Demystify (30 min)**
- What AI actually is, in plain English (a very well-read autocomplete, not a robot brain)
- Live "wow" demo: take a messy real problem from an attendee and solve it live in 3 minutes
- The tool landscape in one slide: ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Copilot — what they are and that the skills transfer between all of them
- What AI is bad at: making things up (hallucination), math, recent events — set honest expectations early

**Part 2 — Prompting that actually works (45 min)**
- The 4-part prompt formula: **Role + Task + Context + Format**
  ("You are an experienced email marketer. Write 3 subject lines for... My audience is... Give me a table with...")
- Iterating: treat it like a conversation with a smart intern, not a search engine
- Hands-on drill #1: everyone rewrites a bad prompt into a great one and compares outputs
- Hands-on drill #2: each attendee brings one real task from their life/job and works it live (this is the moment they get their money's worth)

**— Break (10 min) —**

**Part 3 — The practical playbook (60 min)**
Pick 4–5 based on your audience; do each as a live demo + 5-minute guided practice:
- Writing: emails, social posts, listings, reviews responses, resumes/cover letters
- Summarizing: "read" a 40-page PDF in 2 minutes, meeting notes into action items
- Brainstorming & planning: business ideas, trip itineraries, meal plans, event plans
- Images: generate a logo mockup, social graphic, or product photo concept
- Spreadsheets & data: clean a messy list, draft formulas, analyze simple data
- Voice mode & phone apps: AI while driving/cooking/walking

**Part 4 — Safety, ethics, and staying current (20 min)**
- Privacy: what never to paste into a chatbot (SSNs, client confidential data, passwords)
- How to fact-check AI output; spotting hallucinations
- AI at work: checking your employer's policy, disclosing AI use
- Scams to watch for (voice cloning, fake AI investment schemes)

**Part 5 — Your 30-day plan + upsell (15 min)**
- Each attendee writes down 3 tasks they'll automate this month
- Hand out the **take-home cheat sheet** (prompt formula + 50 copy-paste prompts) — this is your best marketing asset because it gets shared
- Present next steps: your advanced workshop, 1-on-1 consulting, or monthly membership (see §5)

### Teaching method (this is what earns 5-star reviews)

1. **Ratio rule: 30% talking, 70% doing.** Nobody remembers slides; everybody remembers the moment AI wrote their difficult email for them.
2. **Use THEIR problems.** Collect real tasks from attendees at registration ("What's one task you wish you could hand off?") and use those as live demos.
3. **Everyone leaves with 3 wins.** Structure drills so each person completes at least 3 real tasks during the session.
4. **Assume zero tech skill.** Budget 10 minutes at the start for "get everyone logged into a free ChatGPT/Claude account." Have a helper (or arrive early) for account setup stragglers.
5. **Pair up strugglers with faster attendees** — it keeps the room moving and builds energy.
6. **Cap class size at ~15–20** for hands-on quality; larger only with an assistant.
7. **Record nothing the first few times** — iterate the material live, then record once it's tight (that recording becomes an online product later, see §5).

### Logistics checklist
- Venue: library meeting room (often free), coworking space, community center, restaurant back room (Cheffo angle: some restaurants host in exchange for food/drink sales)
- Must-haves: strong Wi-Fi (test it!), projector/TV, power strips
- Attendees bring laptops; have 2–3 loaners or pair people up
- Printed cheat sheet (also emailed after — captures their email)

## 3. Where to sell it — and the Groupon reality check

### Groupon: use it for volume, not profit
Groupon works for workshops, but understand the math before you sign:

- Groupon requires you to discount the offer (typically 40–60% off), **then takes roughly 25–50% commission on the discounted price** (negotiated per merchant; no upfront fee, paid on redemption).
- Example: $99 workshop → listed at $49 on Groupon → Groupon keeps ~$20 → **you net ~$29 per seat**.
- Groupon buyers are deal-hunters and often don't convert to full-price customers.

**The right way to use Groupon:** treat each redeemed voucher as a ~$29 *customer
acquisition*, and make the real money in the room via the back end (§5): the $299
advanced package, consulting, and memberships. Fill the seats you couldn't sell
elsewhere; never rely on it as your main channel. Negotiate the commission —
first-time merchants often accept the first number offered, and it's flexible.

### Better primary channels (keep 90%+ of revenue)

| Channel | Cost to you | Why it works |
|---|---|---|
| **Eventbrite** | ~3.7% + $1.79/ticket + 2.9% processing (~8–14% all-in; can pass to buyer) | Discovery + credibility; people search it for local classes |
| **Meetup** | ~$30/mo organizer fee | Free/paid events; great for building a recurring local audience |
| **Facebook Events + local groups** | Free | Local buzz; boost posts for $20–50 to your zip codes |
| **Nextdoor** | Free | Perfect demographic (curious adults 40+, local small biz) |
| **Community education programs** (community colleges, park districts, libraries) | They pay YOU (flat fee or revenue split) and do all the marketing | Zero-risk income + credibility ("as taught at ___ College") |
| **Chambers of Commerce / Rotary / BNI** | Free–cheap | Direct pipeline to small business owners; often *invite* speakers |
| **Corporate lunch-and-learns** | You charge $500–$2,500 flat | One sale = 25 seats' revenue; pitch HR/L&D at local companies |
| **Your own site (Stripe checkout)** | ~3% | Highest margin; where repeat customers should book |

### Recommended launch sequence
1. **Weeks 1–2:** Run 1–2 free/cheap ($15) pilot sessions via Meetup/library to polish the material and collect testimonials + photos.
2. **Weeks 3–4:** Launch the paid workshop at $79–$99 on Eventbrite + Facebook + Nextdoor. Pitch 2–3 community ed programs (their catalogs book 1–2 quarters ahead — start now).
3. **Month 2:** Add Groupon at $49 to fill remaining seats, with the back-end offer ready.
4. **Month 2–3:** Pitch corporate lunch-and-learns using testimonials from the public sessions.

## 4. Pricing

| Offer | Price |
|---|---|
| Pilot sessions (testimonial-gathering) | Free–$15 |
| Public 3-hour workshop | **$79–$99** ($49 early-bird) |
| Groupon listing | $49 (expect to net ~$25–30) |
| Advanced half-day workshop ("AI for Your Business") | $199–$299 |
| Corporate lunch-and-learn (60–90 min) | $500–$1,000 |
| Corporate half-day training | $1,500–$2,500 |
| 1-on-1 setup & consulting | $100–$150/hr |
| Monthly membership (see §5) | $29–$49/mo |

Don't price the public workshop under $49 outside Groupon — cheap tickets attract
no-shows and signal low value. Early-bird + "bring a friend" discounts beat low base
prices.

## 5. The real money: the ladder behind the workshop

One-off workshop tickets are the top of the funnel, not the business. Each $99
attendee should have an obvious next step:

1. **Workshop ($79–99)** → collect every email, deliver the cheat sheet by email
2. **Advanced workshop ($199–299):** automations, custom GPTs/Claude Projects, AI for marketing, industry-specific sessions (realtors, restaurants, salons — niche sessions can charge *more*)
3. **"Done-with-you" consulting ($100–150/hr):** set up AI tools inside their business — several attendees per cohort will ask for this unprompted
4. **Monthly membership ($29–49/mo):** monthly Zoom Q&A + new prompts + "what changed this month in AI" — recurring revenue that compounds; AI changes fast enough that this genuinely stays valuable
5. **Corporate training ($500–2,500):** attendees who work at companies become your warm intros — end every session with "I also run private team trainings"
6. **Recorded course ($49–99, later):** once the live material is polished, record it and list on your own site + Udemy/Skillshare for passive long-tail income

A realistic early target: 2 public workshops/month × 15 seats × ~$80 avg = ~$2,400,
plus 1 corporate session (~$750), plus consulting spillover — **$3,500–5,000/mo
within a quarter**, before membership revenue builds.

- [ ] Run pilot #1 (free, friends + Meetup); collect testimonials, photos, and a 30-sec phone video

- [ ] Post to Facebook local groups + Nextdoor; $30 boosted post
- [ ] Email 3 community ed programs + 1 chamber of commerce
